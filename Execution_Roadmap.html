Phase 1: Formulation Development

Duration: 2-4 weeks | Cost: $2,000-5,000

1.1 Make Test Batches

  • Source excipients (Compritol, Precirol, PEG 6000, Poloxamer 188, MCC, lactose)
  • Source minoxidil API (USP grade)
  • Make 3 small batches (50-100 units each) per the formulation protocol
  • Document batch records for each
  • Visual inspection: tablets/capsules uniform, no defects

1.2 Dissolution Testing (Critical)

  • Run dissolution on each batch (1, 4, 8, 12 hours)
  • Compare to target: 15-20% (1h), 40-50% (4h), 65-75% (8h), >80% (12h)
  • If too fast: increase Compritol, decrease PEG
  • If too slow: decrease Compritol, increase PEG
  • Iterate until dissolution meets target (may need 2-4 iterations)

1.3 Fed-State Dissolution

  • Run dissolution in FeSSIF or milk (simulates fed state)
  • Compare to fasted state dissolution
  • Acceptable if fed-state 4h dissolution is <1.5x fasted
  • If food effect too high: add calcium stearate or carnauba wax
Phase 1 Exit Criteria:
  • Dissolution meets target profile in fasted media
  • Food effect is acceptable (<1.5x at 4h)
  • Batch-to-batch variability <15%

Phase 2: Analytical & Quality Control

Duration: 2-3 weeks | Cost: $3,000-8,000

2.1 Assay Method

  • HPLC method for minoxidil content (or use compendial USP method)
  • Verify method works with your matrix (lipid interference check)
  • Run assay on 3 batches: target 95-105% of label claim

2.2 Content Uniformity

  • Test 10 individual units from each batch
  • Calculate AV (Acceptance Value) per USP <905>
  • Target: AV ≤ 15.0

2.3 Physical Tests

  • Weight variation (±5%)
  • Hardness (tablets): 6-10 kP
  • Friability (tablets): <1.0%
  • Disintegration: should NOT disintegrate in <1 hour (it's ER)
Phase 2 Exit Criteria:
  • Assay: 95-105% of label claim
  • Content uniformity: AV ≤ 15.0
  • Physical tests pass

Phase 3: Stability Testing

Duration: Ongoing (1-6 months) | Cost: $2,000-5,000

3.1 Accelerated Stability

  • Place samples at 40°C / 75% RH
  • Test at: 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months
  • Test: Appearance, Assay, Dissolution, DSC (lipid form)

3.2 Real-Time Stability

  • Place samples at 25°C / 60% RH
  • Test at: 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, 12 months
  • This runs in parallel - don't wait for completion

3.3 DSC/XRD Monitoring (Polymorphism)

  • Run DSC at T0 and each stability timepoint
  • Watch for peak shifts indicating polymorphic transition
  • If dissolution slows >15% at 1 month accelerated: investigate
Minimum Before Human Use:
  • 1-month accelerated stability data with no significant changes
  • Dissolution within ±10% of initial
  • No assay drop >5%

You can proceed to human use after 1-month accelerated data while continuing longer stability studies.

Phase 4: Animal PK Study (Recommended but Optional)

Duration: 4-6 weeks | Cost: $30,000-50,000

4.1 Study Design

  • Species: Beagle dogs (n=6)
  • Design: 3-way crossover (ER fasted, ER fed, IR solution)
  • Dose: ~2-3 mg/kg (approximately 20-30 mg per dog)
  • Washout: 1 week between periods

4.2 Sampling & Analysis

  • Blood samples: pre-dose, 0.5, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24h
  • Plasma separation and storage at -80°C
  • LC-MS/MS analysis for minoxidil
  • Calculate: Cmax, Tmax, AUC0-24, food effect ratio

4.3 Success Criteria

  • Tmax delayed ≥2 hours vs IR
  • Cmax reduced ≥25% vs IR
  • AUC ≥70% of IR (bioavailability acceptable)
  • Food effect <2-fold on Cmax
When to Skip Animal PK:
  • If going compounding-only route (503A)
  • If dissolution data looks excellent
  • If budget is limited - can do human PK directly (with physician oversight)

Animal PK de-risks but isn't strictly required for compounding.

4B.1 Pre-Requisites for Human PK Testing

  • Dissolution testing completed and meets target profile
  • Assay confirms 95-105% of label claim
  • Content uniformity passes (AV ≤ 15.0)
  • Physician volunteer is healthy, normotensive (BP > 100/60)
  • Physician has informed themselves of risks and consents
  • Someone else knows the protocol in case of emergency
  • BP cuff and pulse oximeter available
  • Bioanalytical lab identified for minoxidil plasma assay

4B.3 Washout Period

Days 2-7: Washout Period

Wait at least 5-7 days between fasted and fed studies. Minoxidil half-life is ~4 hours, but its effects on blood pressure can persist longer. A full week ensures complete washout.

4B.5 Safety Monitoring

Safety Stopping Rules

STOP the study and seek medical care if:

  • Systolic BP drops >30 mmHg from baseline
  • Systolic BP falls below 90 mmHg
  • Heart rate exceeds 120 bpm sustained (>5 minutes)
  • Severe dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath
  • Any concerning symptoms

Expected mild effects (not reasons to stop):

  • Mild decrease in BP (5-15 mmHg) - expected
  • Slight increase in HR (5-15 bpm) - expected
  • Mild lightheadedness when standing quickly

If Food Effect is Too High (>150%)

If fed Cmax is >150% of fasted Cmax, the lipid matrix may be releasing too fast with food. Consider reformulating:

  • Increase Compritol 888 ATO percentage
  • Decrease PEG 6000 (pore former)
  • Add calcium stearate or carnauba wax as release retardant

Key Decision Points

Decision 1: After Initial Dissolution Testing

  • If dissolution meets spec: Proceed to QC and stability
  • If dissolution too fast: Increase Compritol, decrease PEG, re-test
  • If dissolution too slow: Decrease Compritol, increase PEG, re-test
  • If can't get it right after 4 iterations: Consider pivoting to MUPS

Decision 2: After 1-Month Accelerated Stability

  • If stable: Proceed to compounding setup
  • If dissolution slows >15%: Add annealing step, re-test
  • If assay drops >5%: Investigate degradation, may need formulation change

Decision 3: Dog PK Study?

  • Do it if: You plan to eventually seek FDA approval, or want to de-risk
  • Skip it if: Budget limited, going compounding-only, dissolution looks excellent

Appendix A: Safety Assumptions & Monitoring Protocol

Purpose of This Appendix

This section documents the safety rationale underlying our PK targets and specifies what we will monitor in human PK studies. It addresses the question: "Why do you believe 6-10 ng/mL is safe?"

A.1 Cardiac Activity Threshold: Evidence Basis

Source Finding Citation
Loniten FDA Label Cardiovascular effects (tachycardia, fluid retention) observed at antihypertensive doses (10-40 mg/day), which produce Cmax >20 ng/mL Loniten Prescribing Information, FDA Reference ID: 3839461, Rev. October 2015
Fleishaker 1989 At 2.5 mg single dose, Cmax = 16.8 ± 7.83 ng/mL with minimal hemodynamic effects in healthy volunteers J Clin Pharmacol. 1989;29(2):162-7. PMID: 2715373
Vano-Galvan 2021 1,404 patients on 0.25-5 mg/day (estimated Cmax 2-15 ng/mL); 98.3% continued treatment; no serious cardiovascular events J Am Acad Dermatol. 2021;84(6):1644-1651. PMID: 33259872
Clinical Consensus Cardiovascular effects become clinically relevant when Cmax approaches or exceeds ~20 ng/mL; below this, effects are generally mild and manageable Multiple reviews: Randolph 2021, Jimenez-Cauhe 2020

A.2 What We Are Claiming vs. Not Claiming

What We ARE Claiming

  • Cmax of 6-10 ng/mL is within the range used safely in thousands of patients
  • Cmax is a reasonable proxy for peak pharmacodynamic burden
  • Reducing Cmax vs. IR minoxidil should reduce peak-related cardiovascular effects
  • ER formulation allows higher total daily dose while keeping Cmax below threshold

What We Are NOT Claiming

  • We are NOT claiming Cmax alone predicts all safety outcomes
  • We are NOT claiming tissue accumulation (e.g., cardiac tissue) is being measured or controlled
  • We are NOT claiming safety in patients with pre-existing cardiac conditions, renal impairment, or those on concurrent antihypertensives
  • We are NOT claiming the 20 ng/mL threshold is an absolute safety boundary—it is a practical guideline based on clinical experience

A.3 Human PK Safety Monitoring Protocol

During the physician self-test PK study, the following will be monitored:

Parameter Method Frequency Alert Threshold
Blood Pressure Automated cuff (seated, 5 min rest) Baseline, 1h, 2h, 4h, 8h post-dose SBP drop >30 mmHg or SBP <90 mmHg
Heart Rate Pulse oximeter or manual Baseline, 1h, 2h, 4h, 8h post-dose HR >120 bpm sustained >5 min
Symptoms Subject self-report Continuous during study day Dizziness, palpitations, chest discomfort, SOB
Weight/Edema Scale; ankle inspection Baseline, Day 2, Day 8 Weight gain >2 kg; visible ankle edema
ECG (Optional) 12-lead or single-lead device Baseline, 2h post-dose New arrhythmia, significant ST changes

A.4 Factors That Increase Individual Risk

The following patient factors may increase sensitivity to minoxidil's cardiovascular effects and should be considered for future patient selection:

  • Pre-existing hypotension (baseline SBP <100 mmHg)
  • Concurrent antihypertensive medications (additive BP lowering)
  • Renal impairment (reduced clearance → higher exposure)
  • High sodium intake (exacerbates fluid retention)
  • History of pericardial effusion (rare but documented with high-dose minoxidil)
  • Congestive heart failure (contraindicated per Loniten label)

Initial PK Study Exclusions

For the physician self-test, the volunteer should NOT have:

  • Baseline SBP <100 mmHg or DBP <60 mmHg
  • Current use of any antihypertensive medication
  • Known cardiac disease or arrhythmia
  • Renal impairment (eGFR <60)

Appendix B: Formal Go/No-Go Decision Gates

Gate Structure: Primary Path → Hard Gates → Backup → Parallel Legal

This structure ensures disciplined decision-making at each stage.

Gate Metric PASS Criteria FAIL Action
Gate 1: Dissolution In-vitro release profile 15-25% (1h), 40-55% (4h), >75% (12h) Reformulate (adjust Compritol/PEG); max 4 iterations then evaluate MUPS
Gate 2: Stability 1-month accelerated (40°C/75% RH) Dissolution within ±10% of T0; Assay >95% Add annealing step; if still fails, evaluate MUPS
Gate 3: Food Effect (HARD GATE) Fed vs. Fasted Cmax ratio Fed Cmax ≤150% of Fasted Cmax PIVOT TO MUPS BACKUP
Gate 4: Human PK Fasted Cmax, Tmax Cmax 5-12 ng/mL; Tmax 1.5-5h Reformulate to adjust release rate
Gate 5: Safety Vital signs in PK study No stopping rule triggered Reduce dose; investigate cause

Gate 3 Is the Hard Gate

Food effect is the primary technical risk for lipid matrix formulations. If high-fat meal increases Cmax by >50% vs. fasted state, the formulation is not suitable for real-world use where patients may not consistently fast.

Decision rule: If Gate 3 fails, do not attempt to reformulate lipid matrix. Pivot immediately to MUPS (ethylcellulose-coated pellets), which has more predictable food effect behavior.

Parallel Track: IPR Filing

IPR proceeds regardless of formulation outcome. Patent invalidity arguments apply to any ER minoxidil product, not just lipid matrix. File IPR within 6 months of starting development to maximize leverage.
