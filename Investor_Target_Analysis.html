Competitive Intelligence Notes

Why OrbiMed Likely Passed on Veradermics

OrbiMed is the largest healthcare-focused VC with $19B AUM. Their absence from Veradermics' cap table (despite being in virtually every major healthcare IPO) suggests they may have concerns about:

  • Patent vulnerability (same concerns we've identified)
  • Valuation (Veradermics raised at high valuations)
  • Competition risk (generic minoxidil is $4/month)
  • Reimbursement uncertainty (cosmetic vs. medical indication)

Pitch angle: "We address the concerns you likely had about Veradermics - different IP, capital-efficient path, proven drug."

Why Eli Lilly Took a 4.9% Stake

Eli Lilly's investment signals Big Pharma interest in the hair loss space. However, 4.9% is a "watching" position, not a committed acquirer. This creates opportunity:

  • Other Big Pharma may want competitive exposure
  • If Lilly acquires Veradermics, there's a market gap for alternatives
  • Lilly's interest validates the market opportunity
