1. Why This Works

The Clinical Opportunity

Oral minoxidil for hair loss is already proven effective:

Study Patients Response Rate Safety
Vano-Galvan 2021 1,404 98.3% continued treatment No serious adverse events
Jimenez-Cauhe 2020 442 70-100% Well-tolerated
Randolph & Tosti 2021 634 (review) "Effective treatment" "Well-tolerated"

The Problem with IR Minoxidil

Immediate-release minoxidil at 5mg produces Cmax of ~22 ng/mL, exceeding the cardiac activity threshold (~20 ng/mL). This causes side effects:

  • Tachycardia (rapid heart rate)
  • Hypotension (low blood pressure)
  • Fluid retention

Solution: Extended-release formulation reduces peak levels while maintaining therapeutic exposure.

The PK Targets

Parameter Target Rationale
Cmax 6-10 ng/mL Below cardiac threshold (20 ng/mL), above hair growth threshold (1.62 ng/mL)
Tmax 2-4 hours Delayed peak reduces side effects
Duration >1.62 ng/mL >8 hours Sustained therapeutic coverage

2. Technical Approach: Lipid Matrix

Veradermics (Patented)

Technology: HPMC gel matrix

  • Hydrophilic polymer
  • Swells in water (2-4x volume)
  • Forms viscous gel layer
  • Drug diffuses through gel

Release modifier: HPMC K4M, K200M (50-80% w/w)

Our Approach (Lipid Matrix)

Technology: Lipid matrix (Compritol/Precirol)

  • Hydrophobic lipids
  • Does NOT swell
  • NO gel formation
  • Drug releases via erosion/pore diffusion

Release modifier: Glyceryl behenate, glyceryl palmitostearate

Why Lipid Matrix?

  1. Strongest patent defense - Lipids are NOT cellulose derivatives; completely different chemistry
  2. Simple manufacturing - Melt granulation with standard equipment
  3. Lower cost - Cheaper excipients, simpler process
  4. Established technology - Compritol/Precirol used for 30+ years

3. Legal Strategy

Patent Landscape

US10226462B2 Samson Clinical Immediate-release only 0.1-0.49 mg LOW (ER, different dose)
Our Product N/A Lipid matrix ER 8.5 mg (x2) N/A

Two-Pronged Defense Strategy

Prong 1: Non-Infringement

  • Lipids are NOT "release modifiers" as defined in Veradermics' specification
  • Different chemical class (glycerides vs. cellulose ethers)
  • Different release mechanism (erosion vs. gel diffusion)
  • No gel formation, no swelling - fundamentally different behavior

Prong 2: Patent Invalidity (IPR)

  • Prior art IR minoxidil achieves the claimed PK parameters (Fleishaker 1989)
  • Oral minoxidil for hair loss was known (1,400+ patients pre-2022)
  • Making ER from known IR drug is routine optimization (KSR v. Teleflex)
  • No unexpected results - ER reducing Cmax is the definition of ER

Condensed Claim Chart: Our Formulation vs. Veradermics Claims

Veradermics Claim Element Our Lipid Matrix Status
"Modified release pharmaceutical formulation" Yes - extended release MEETS (broadly)
"Release modifier" (spec: HPMC, cellulose derivatives) Lipids (Compritol/Precirol) - NOT cellulose DOES NOT MEET
"Release modifier at 50-80% w/w" (Claim 12) Lipid at 57% - but different chemistry DOES NOT MEET (different class)
"Gel-forming" / "swelling" behavior (spec) No gel, no swelling - erosion mechanism DOES NOT MEET
Cmax 0.25-20 ng/mL Target 6-10 ng/mL MEETS (but so does IR prior art)
Tmax 30-360 minutes Target 120-240 min MEETS (but so does IR prior art)
Dose 3.5-20 mg 8.5 mg (x2 daily) MEETS

Claim Chart Summary

Non-infringement argument: We don't meet the "release modifier" limitation as defined by the specification (HPMC/cellulose derivatives). Lipids are a fundamentally different chemical class with different mechanism.

Invalidity argument: The PK parameters we "meet" (Cmax, Tmax, dose) are also met by prior art IR minoxidil, making those limitations non-novel.

Fallback if broad construction: If court construes "release modifier" broadly to include any ER technology, we pivot to invalidity + argue the "way" is different under doctrine of equivalents (no gel, no swelling).

Key Legal Arguments

4. Execution Roadmap

1
Formulation Development (Weeks 1-4)
Make test batches, run dissolution testing, iterate until profile meets spec. Cost: $2-5K
2
Analytical & QC (Weeks 2-4)
Assay (95-105%), content uniformity (AV ≤15), physical tests. Cost: $3-8K
3
Stability Testing (Weeks 4+ ongoing)
Accelerated (40°C/75% RH) and real-time stability. Need 1-month data before human use. Cost: $2-5K
4
Human PK Testing (Weeks 6-8)
Physician self-test: fasted and fed PK profiles. 9 blood draws each. Cost: $1.5-4K
5
Compounding Setup (Weeks 5-8)
Tech transfer to pharmacy, qualification batch, prescriber setup. Cost: $1-3K

Formal Decision Gates

Gate Criteria If FAIL
Gate 1: Dissolution 15-25% (1h), 40-55% (4h), >75% (12h) Reformulate; max 4 iterations
Gate 2: Stability 1-month accelerated: dissolution ±10%, assay >95% Add annealing; if still fails, evaluate MUPS
Gate 3: FOOD EFFECT (HARD) Fed Cmax ≤150% of Fasted Cmax PIVOT TO MUPS BACKUP
Gate 4: Human PK Fasted Cmax 5-12 ng/mL; Tmax 1.5-5h Adjust release rate

Gate 3 is non-negotiable. If food effect exceeds 150%, lipid matrix is unsuitable for real-world use. Pivot immediately to MUPS.

Additional Milestones

  • Go/No-Go #1: Dissolution meets 15-20% (1h), 40-50% (4h), >80% (12h)
  • Go/No-Go #2: 1-month accelerated stability shows no significant change
  • Go/No-Go #3: Human PK shows Cmax 6-10 ng/mL, Tmax 2-4h
