Lipid Matrix Extended-Release Minoxidil

Formulation Development Protocol

Document Version: 1.0

Date: February 5, 2026

Confidential - For Development Use Only

1. Executive Summary

This protocol describes the development of an extended-release oral minoxidil formulation using lipid matrix technology. The target product is an 8.5 mg tablet/capsule dosed twice daily (BID) for a total daily dose of 17 mg.

Target indication: Hair loss (androgenetic alopecia) - prescribed off-label

Release mechanism: Lipid matrix erosion

Key advantage: Different mechanism from HPMC-based competitors (patent differentiation)

4. Manufacturing Process

4.1 Process Overview: Melt Granulation

Method: Hot melt granulation followed by direct compression (tablets) or capsule filling

Scale: Protocol written for 100-unit batch (25g); scale as needed

Equipment needed:

  • Jacketed mixing vessel or hot plate with temperature control
  • Overhead stirrer or planetary mixer
  • Calibrated thermometer or temperature probe
  • Analytical balance (0.01g precision minimum)
  • Granulator/mill (oscillating or conical)
  • Tablet press or capsule filling machine

4.2 Step-by-Step Manufacturing Protocol

1 Pre-Weigh All Components

Weigh all components according to batch formula. Record actual weights.

Poloxamer 188500 mg_____ mg
Colloidal silicon dioxide500 mg_____ mg
Magnesium stearate650 mg_____ mg

2 Prepare Lipid Melt

  1. Add Compritol 888 ATO and Precirol ATO 5 to jacketed vessel
  2. Heat to 75-80°C with gentle stirring (50 rpm)
  3. Once melted, add PEG 6000 and Poloxamer 188
  4. Continue stirring until homogeneous melt (clear to slightly hazy)
  5. Maintain temperature at 75 ± 2°C

CHECKPOINT Melt should be uniform with no solid particles visible

Critical Parameters:
  • Do NOT exceed 85°C - lipid degradation may occur
  • Do NOT allow temperature to drop below 70°C during drug addition
  • Mixing speed should be gentle to avoid air incorporation

3 Add Active Ingredient

  1. Pre-blend minoxidil with a small portion of MCC (approximately 500 mg MCC) in a separate container
  2. Slowly add the minoxidil/MCC blend to the lipid melt while stirring
  3. Mix for 5 minutes at 75°C to ensure uniform dispersion
  4. Scrape vessel walls to ensure no drug adheres to sides

CHECKPOINT No visible drug aggregates; uniform dispersion

4 Add Remaining Fillers

  1. With continued stirring, add remaining MCC and lactose monohydrate
  2. Mix for 3-5 minutes until uniformly distributed
  3. Mass should become thick paste-like consistency

5 Cooling and Solidification

  1. Remove vessel from heat source OR begin cooling jacket circulation
  2. Continue slow stirring (30 rpm) as mass cools
  3. Allow to cool to 40-45°C (mass will become semi-solid)
  4. Transfer to trays and allow to cool to room temperature (20-25°C)
  5. Rest for minimum 2 hours (overnight preferred) for complete solidification

CHECKPOINT Mass should be solid but not brittle

Optional Annealing Step (Recommended):

To stabilize lipid polymorphic form and improve shelf-life stability:

  1. After initial solidification, place solid mass in oven at 50°C for 24 hours
  2. This accelerates transition to stable β-polymorph
  3. Cool to room temperature before proceeding

6 Granulation/Milling

  1. Break solid mass into smaller chunks manually
  2. Pass through oscillating granulator or conical mill
  3. Target granule size: 250-500 μm
  4. Screen through 20-mesh sieve (850 μm) to remove oversized particles
  5. Screen through 60-mesh sieve (250 μm) to remove fines (optional)

CHECKPOINT Free-flowing granules with minimal fines

7 Final Blending

  1. Transfer granules to V-blender or bin blender
  2. Add colloidal silicon dioxide
  3. Blend for 3 minutes
  4. Add magnesium stearate
  5. Blend for 2 minutes only (over-blending reduces tablet hardness)

CHECKPOINT Uniform blend; good flow properties

8 Compression or Encapsulation

Option A: Tablets

  • Compress to target weight: 250 mg ± 5%
  • Target hardness: 6-10 kP (adjust based on friability)
  • Tooling: 8-9 mm round, standard concave

Option B: Capsules

  • Fill Size 1 or Size 0 hard gelatin or HPMC capsules
  • Target fill weight: 250 mg ± 5%

6.2 Dissolution Method and Specifications

Apparatus: USP Apparatus 2 (Paddle)

Medium: 900 mL phosphate buffer pH 6.8

Temperature: 37.0 ± 0.5°C

Speed: 50 rpm

Sampling: 1, 2, 4, 8, 12 hours

Detection: UV at 285 nm or HPLC

Time Point Specification Target (Optimal) Rationale
1 hour 10-25% 15-20% Reduced initial release for lower Cmax
2 hours 20-40% 25-35% Gradual increase
4 hours 35-60% 40-50% Sustained release matching Veradermics profile
8 hours 55-80% 65-75% Maintain levels above hair growth threshold
12 hours NLT 80% ≥85% Complete release by next dose
Target PK Rationale (based on Veradermics clinical data):
  • Veradermics VDPHL01 achieves Cmax ~7-8 ng/mL - our target is 6-10 ng/mL
  • Hair growth threshold: 1.62 ng/mL - must maintain above this for 10-12 hours
  • Cardiac threshold: 20 ng/mL - must stay well below to avoid CV side effects
  • Slower initial release = lower Cmax = better tolerability
Fed-State Dissolution (Additional Test):

To assess food effect risk, also test in:

  • Medium: FeSSIF (Fed State Simulated Intestinal Fluid) or milk
  • Acceptance: 4-hour dissolution should not exceed fasted state by more than 30%

Critical Stability Alert - Lipid Polymorphism:

The most likely stability failure is a change in dissolution due to lipid polymorphic transitions (α → β' → β). Monitor closely:

  • If dissolution slows by >15% at accelerated conditions, investigate DSC
  • Annealing step (50°C/24h) during manufacturing helps prevent this
  • Consider adding 5-10% liquid lipid (Miglyol 812) to disrupt crystal packing if stability fails

8. Formulation Troubleshooting Guide

8.1 Release Too Fast

Symptom Likely Cause Solution
>35% at 1 hour Insufficient lipid or drug on surface Increase Compritol to 45-50%; ensure thorough mixing
Burst release then plateau Drug on granule surface Pre-blend drug with MCC before adding to melt
Fast release with food/FeSSIF Bile salt sensitivity Add 2-3% calcium stearate or 3-5% carnauba wax
Batch-to-batch variability Temperature inconsistency Tighten process controls; use jacketed vessel

8.2 Release Too Slow

Symptom Likely Cause Solution
<10% at 1 hour Poor wetting Increase Poloxamer 188 to 3-4%
<80% at 12 hours Drug trapped in lipid Increase PEG 6000 to 12-15%
Slows on stability Polymorphic transition Add annealing step; consider liquid lipid addition
Incomplete release (<85% at 24h) Excess lipid content Reduce total lipid to 50%; increase hydrophilic fillers

8.3 Physical Issues

Problem Likely Cause Solution
Poor flow Excess fines or sticky granules Increase silicon dioxide to 3%; ensure complete cooling before milling
Sticking to punches Low melting lipid softening Reduce Precirol; increase Compritol; keep compression area cool
Capping/lamination Air entrapment or excess lubricant Reduce mixing speed during melt; reduce mag stearate blend time
Weight variation Poor granule flow Improve particle size distribution; increase glidant

9. Scale-Up Considerations

Critical Scale-Up Parameters:

  • Heat transfer: Larger batches have temperature gradients; use jacketed equipment with good mixing
  • Mixing efficiency: Impeller design and speed must be scaled appropriately
  • Cooling rate: Larger batches cool more slowly; may affect crystal form
  • Granulation: Mill capacity and screen size may need adjustment
Batch Size Equipment Considerations
Lab (25-100g) Hot plate + beaker or small jacketed vessel Manual process; good for development
Pilot (1-5 kg) Jacketed planetary mixer or high-shear granulator with heating Validate process parameters; establish CPPs
Production (10-100 kg) Large-scale melt granulator (e.g., Lödige) or twin-screw extruder May switch to continuous processing; full process validation required

10. Preclinical Testing Recommendations

10.1 Dog Pharmacokinetic Study (Recommended Before Human Trials)

Objective: Characterize PK profile and food effect in relevant animal model

Species: Beagle dogs (n=6, crossover design)

Dose: 2-3 mg/kg (approximately 20-30 mg per dog)

Arms:

  1. ER lipid matrix formulation - fasted
  2. ER lipid matrix formulation - fed (high-fat meal)
  3. IR minoxidil solution - fasted (reference)

Sampling: Pre-dose, 0.5, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24 hours

Analysis: Validated LC-MS/MS method for minoxidil in plasma

Endpoints: Cmax, Tmax, AUC0-24, food effect ratio

Success Criteria:
  • ER formulation shows delayed Tmax (≥2 hours) vs. IR solution
  • ER formulation shows reduced Cmax (≥30% lower) vs. IR at equivalent dose
  • Food effect on Cmax is <2-fold
  • AUC is ≥70% of IR solution (acceptable bioavailability)
