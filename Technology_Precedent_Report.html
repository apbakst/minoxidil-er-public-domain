Technology Precedent Report

Lipid Matrix Extended-Release Oral Formulations

Date: February 5, 2026

Subject: Established precedent for lipid matrix technology in pharmaceutical products

Application: Extended-release minoxidil 8.5 mg oral tablet/capsule

Executive Summary

Lipid matrix technology using glyceryl behenate (Compritol 888 ATO) and glyceryl palmitostearate (Precirol ATO 5) is a well-established pharmaceutical platform with:

  • FDA-approved products using lipid-based solid oral dosage forms (e.g., Kaletra)
  • 30+ years of commercial use of Compritol and Precirol excipients
  • Extensive peer-reviewed literature demonstrating safety and efficacy
  • Compendial status in USP-NF, PhEur, and JP
  • Proven manufacturing scalability from lab to commercial production

This formulation approach represents low technical risk based on established precedent.

4. Cardiovascular Drug ER Precedents

Minoxidil is a vasodilator originally developed for hypertension. Multiple cardiovascular drugs have been successfully converted from immediate-release to extended-release formulations:

Drug Class IR Product ER Product Technology
Beta-blocker Lopressor (metoprolol tartrate) Toprol-XL (metoprolol succinate) HPMC matrix
Beta-blocker Inderal (propranolol) Inderal LA Coated pellets
Calcium channel blocker Procardia (nifedipine) Procardia XL Osmotic pump (OROS)
Calcium channel blocker Cardizem (diltiazem) Cardizem CD/LA Coated beads / HPMC
Calcium channel blocker Calan (verapamil) Calan SR, Verelan HPMC matrix / pellets
ACE inhibitor Vasotec (enalapril) Various ER generics Matrix systems
Vasodilator Loniten (minoxidil) Our ER formulation Lipid matrix

Key Point: Converting cardiovascular drugs from IR to ER is a well-established regulatory and commercial pathway. Our minoxidil ER follows this proven model.

9. Conclusion

Key Takeaways

  1. Proven Technology: Lipid matrix extended-release is a mature pharmaceutical platform with FDA-approved products (Kaletra) and 30+ years of commercial use.
  2. Established Excipients: Compritol 888 ATO and Precirol ATO 5 are compendial (USP-NF), GRAS-listed, and widely used in approved pharmaceutical products.
  3. Published Precedent: Extensive peer-reviewed literature supports the use of these exact excipients for sustained-release oral formulations.
  4. Cardiovascular Precedent: Multiple cardiovascular drugs have been successfully converted from IR to ER formulations.
  5. Low Technical Risk: The proposed formulation uses established technology, approved excipients, and standard manufacturing processes.

This formulation represents an application of proven technology, not experimental science.
